Courses

An overview of all the courses I have taken.

Computer Engineering & Software Courses

  • ENPH 459, Engineering Physics Project II: Project planning, management and reporting. This course involves writing a project proposal, carrying out an open-ended software project, and reporting the results both orally and in writing.
  • APSC 486, New Venture Design: Teams comprising three engineering and three business students create a business plan and a prototype or engineering solution of a novel product, process, or process component.
  • ENPH 353, Engineering Physics Project I: Learned Python, computer vision, machine learning, Linux environments.
  • CPEN 221, Principals of Software Construction: Design, implementation, reasoning about software systems: abstraction and specification of software, testing, verification, abstract data types, object-oriented design, type hierarchies, concurrent software design all programmed in Java.
  • APSC 160, Introduction to Computation in Engineering Design: Introduction to programming, all in C programming language.
  • ENPH 253, Introduction to Instrument Design: Programmed robot movements in C++ using Microcontrollers. Used a state machine design, and implemented master-slave communication between microcontrollers.

    • Electrical Engineering & Hardware Courses

  • ELEC 341, Systems and Control: Continuous time system analysis by Laplace transforms; system modelling by transfer function and state space methods; feedback, stability and sensitivity; control design; frequency domain analysis.
  • ELEC 401, Analog CMOS Integrated Circuit Design: Design and analysis of analog integrated circuits, with emphasis on CMOS design techniques. Gain stages, opamp design, frequency compensation, oscillators, A/D, D/A converters, PLL, DLL.
  • CPEN 312, Digital Systems and Microcomputers: Learned about boolean algebra, design, optimization and implementation of combinatorial and sequential circuits, memory and programmable logic devices. Learned assembly hardware description language.
  • ELEC 221, Signals and Systems: Learned about convolution sum, discrete-time Fourier series and transforms, z-transform, sampling, introduction to filtering and modulation, feedback systems and stability.
  • ENPH 259, Experimental Techniques: Built RLC circuits, analog-to-digital converters and logic circuits.
  • ENPH 253, Introduction to Instrument Design: Built circuits for a robot including H-bridges, de-bouncing circuits, and power boards. Worked with sensors including QRD reflectance sensors and sonars.
  • ELEC 204, Linear Circuits: Learned about Bode plots and the Laplace transform, treatment of RLC circuits, phasors, op-amps, diodes and BJT's.

    • Math Courses

  • MATH 400, Applied Partial Differential Equations: Separation of variables, first order equations, Sturm-Liouville theory, integral transform methods.
  • MATH 318, Probability with Physical Applications: Random variables, discrete and continuous distributions. Random walk, Markov chains, Monte Carlo methods. Characteristic functions, limit laws.
  • MATH 307, Applied Linear Algebra: Applications of linear algebra to problems in science and engineering; use of computer algebra systems for solving problems in linear algebra.
  • MATH 305, Applied Complex Analysis: Functions of a complex variable, Cauchy-Riemann equations, contour integration, Laurent series, residues, integrals of multi-valued functions, Fourier transforms.
  • MATH 257, Partial Differential Equations: Introduction to partial differential equations, Fourier series, the heat, wave and potential equations, boundary-value problems, and numerical methods.
  • MATH 217, Multivariable and Vector Calculus: Partial differentiation, extreme values, multiple integration, vector fields, line and surface integrals, the divergence theorem, Green's and Stokes' theorems.
  • MATH 255, Ordinary Differential Equations: Review of linear systems, nonlinear equations and applications, phase plane analysis, Laplace transforms, and numerical methods.
  • MATH 152, Linear Systems: 2D and 3D geometry, vectors and matrices, eigenvalues and vibration, physical applications. Laboratories demonstrate computer solutions of large systems.
  • MATH 101, Integral Calculus: The definite integral, integration techniques, applications, modelling, infinite series.
  • MATH 100, Differential Calculus: Derivatives of elementary functions. Applications and modelling: graphing, optimization.

    • Physics Courses

  • PHYS 401, Electromagnetic Theory: Applications of Maxwell's theory. Wave propagation in dielectrics, conductors and plasmas, wave guides, radiation, antennae, and special relativity.
  • PHYS 408, Optics: Principles and applications of optical physics. Interference, diffraction, coherence, polarization, Fresnel relations, optical coatings, waves in dielectric media, Gaussian beams, waveguides, optical cavities, lasers, fibre optics, and Fourier optics.
  • PHYS 403, Statistical Mechanics: Principles and applications of statistical mechanics. Ideal gases, degenerate Fermi gases, Bose-Einstein condensation, black body radiation, fluctuations and phase transitions.
  • PHYS 410, Computational Physics: Scientific programming applied to problems in physics. Fundamentals of numerical analysis for continuum problems. Solution of linear and non-linear algebraic systems, ordinary differential equations and stochastic problems.
  • ENPH 352, Laboratory Techniques in Physics: Experimental physics focussing on topics learned from previous physics courses.
  • PHYS 301, Electricity and Magnetism: Maxwell's equations and their applications, electrical fields and potentials of static charge distributions, current, fields of moving charges, magnetic fields, electromagnetic induction.
  • PHYS 304, Introduction to Quantum Mechanics: Principles and applications of quantum mechanics, wave mechanics, the Schroedinger equation, expectation values, Hermitian operators, commuting observables, one-dimensional systems, harmonic oscillators, angular momentum, three dimensional systems.
  • PHYS 350, Applications of Classical Mechanics: Particle mechanics: Euler's equations, tops and gyroscopes, motion of the Earth, Lagrangian and Hamiltonian methods. Variational principles in optics and mechanics, Liouville's theorem and statistical mechanics. The relationship between classical and quantum mechanics.
  • PHYS 250, Introduction to Modern Physics: Wave-particle duality of matter, special relativity, processes in atomic, nuclear and solid state, and introduction to quantum mechanical devices and techniques.
  • ENPH 257, Heat and Thermodynamics: Thermometry, thermal properties of matter, heat transfer by conduction, convection and radiation, kinetic theory of gases and gas laws, heat engines, refrigeration, change of state, first and second laws of thermodynamics.
  • ENPH 270, Mechanics II: Dynamics: systems of particles, kinematics and kinetics of rigid bodies (plane motion), energy and momentum, rotating coordinates.
  • PHYS 170, Mechanics I: Statics of particles, equilibrium or rigid bodies, rigid body statics and internal forces, trusses. Kinematics: rectilinear motion. Dynamics: Newton's second law, friction, impulse, momentum, work and energy.
  • PHYS 159, Physics Laboratory: A laboratory course with emphasis on experimental design, measurement and analysis techniques.
  • PHYS 158, Introductory Physics for Engineers II: Electricity and magnetism, DC and AC circuits, optics.
  • PHYS 157, Introductory Physics for Engineers I: Heat, thermodynamics, oscillations, waves, and sound.

    • Entrepreneurship Courses

  • COMM 382, Economics of Entrepreneurship and Innovation: Fundamental economic issues faced by entrepreneurs who want to commercialize innovations within start-ups and established firms.
  • ECON 310, Principles of Microeconomics: Elements of theory concerning the economics of markets and market behaviour, prices and costs, exchange and trade, competition and monopoly, distribution of income.
  • APSC 486, New Venture Design: Teams comprising three engineering and three business students create a business plan and a prototype or engineering solution of a novel product, process, or process component.
  • COMM 280, Entrepreneurship: Entrepreneurial essentials from creating viable opportunities through building a founding team, strategic planning, going to market, fleshing out an organization and planning the future of the business.
  • COMR 482, New Product Development: How new products are developed and the factors that influence success and failure throughout the various stages of development.
  • COMR 387, Entrepreneurial Finance: Financial issues encountered by those active on the investing side of entrepreneurship, such as working for banks, venture capital firms, or corporate venture organizations, or anyone interacting with entrepreneurs and private equity investors
  • ELEC 481, Economic Analysis of Engineering Projects: Time-money relationships; economic analysis of alternatives including the effects of interest rates, inflation, depreciation, taxation and uncertainty; cost estimation and budgeting; financial analysis of engineering operations.

    • Mechanical, Civil & Materials Engineering Courses

  • MECH 325, Machine Design: Learned about selection of flexible drives, bearings, fluid power system components, and couplings. Design of bolted joints and power screws. Design and selection of gears, gear trains, and mechanisms.
  • MECH 360, Mechanics of Materials: Learned about beam deflections, singularity functions, column buckling, Castigliano's theorem, statically indeterminate beams, shear centre, principal stresses and stress invariants in three dimensions.
  • MECH 280, Introduction to Fluid Mechanics: Learned about Fluid properties, statics, kinematics, dynamics, energy, and momentum principles for control volumes. Applied dimensional analysis and similarity; laminar and turbulent flow; pipe flow; principles of centrifugal pumps.
  • MECH 260, Introduction to Mechanics of Materials: Statically determinate trusses, normal and shear stresses and strains, shear force and bending moment diagrams, Mohr's circle and yield and ultimate failure criteria.
  • APSC 278, Engineering Materials: Atomic bonding, crystal structures and imperfections, properties of metals, ceramics, polymers, wood, concrete and fibre composite materials, selection of materials, corrosion, mechanical testing and heat treatment.
  • APSC 279, Engineering Materials Laboratory: Atomic bonding, crystal structures and imperfections, properties of metals, ceramics, polymers, wood, concrete and fibre composite materials, selection of materials, corrosion, mechanical testing and heat treatment.
  • CIVL 250, Engineering and Sustainable Development:Implications of a finite biosphere and the complexities inherent in environmental decision-making.
  • APSC 450, Professional Engineering Practice:Legislation affecting the practice of engineering; ethical principles and responsibilities. Management of engineering enterprises; labour relations, safety and environmental legislation.
